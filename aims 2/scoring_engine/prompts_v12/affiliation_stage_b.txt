# Classification Rules

**Affiliation Imagery (AffIm)** is scored when the story contains evidence of concern in one or more characters over **establishing, maintaining, or restoring a positive affective relationship (friendship)** with another person or group.

THREE bases for scoring AffIm — only ONE needs to be met:
1. **Statement of friendly relationship** — explicit warmth/companionship language ("buddies," "close friends," "loving family," "warm relationship"). Mere relationship labels ("father-son," "lovers," "married couple") do NOT qualify without further warmth elaboration.
2. **Statement of affiliative feeling or wanting** — explicit liking, desire to be liked/accepted, sorrow at separation, joy at reunion. Reactions to disrupted relationships count when there is evidence of WANTING restoration.
3. **Companionate / affiliative activity** — parties, reunions, visits, bull sessions, friendly nurturance NOT prescribed by role obligation.

# What Does NOT Qualify

- Routine role behavior (parent disciplining child, teacher teaching, fraternity policy meeting)
- Mere relationship labels without warmth ("father and son," "lovers," "married couple")
- Convivial settings explicitly counter-indicated (a fraternity gathering described as a debate; a "family meeting" that is purely transactional)
- Heterosexual dating/sex without affiliative content (sex per se is not affiliation)
- Achievement or Power overlapping (when scientific work, professional success, or impact-on-others is the dominant concern)
- Routine professional caregiving (doctor-patient, teacher-student) when no warmth beyond role duty is shown

# Key Distinctions for Borderline Cases

1. **Role label vs warmth statement**: "Father-daughter" does NOT qualify without additional language ("close throughout life," "so glad to see her"). A label alone is not affiliation.
2. **Companionate setting vs counter-indicated**: A reunion with warmth = AffIm=1. A "reunion" that turns into a policy debate or business meeting = AffIm=0.
3. **Obligation test**: A father's nurturance toward his own child is parental duty; the same nurturance between non-relatives indicates affiliation. Unless the story makes clear the act goes beyond role obligation.
4. **Heterosexual dating**: Dating behavior may or may not imply affiliation. Look for explicit warmth, companionship, friendship language. Sexual content alone is not affiliation.
5. **Marriage**: Concern over marriage NORMALLY implies more than sex and IS scored AffIm UNLESS counter-elaborated.
6. **Disrupted relationship**: Mere mention of a broken relationship is NOT affiliation. There must be evidence someone WANTS the relationship restored.

# Decision Framework

When providers are split, ask:
1. Can I identify a SPECIFIC affiliation concern (not just a relationship label)?
2. Is at least one character expressing or showing concern over establishing/maintaining/restoring a warm relationship?
3. If the setting is companionate (reunion, party), is the affiliative nature counter-indicated by elaboration?
4. Does the story go beyond role obligation in showing affiliative concern?

If YES to 1, 2 and the setting is not counter-indicated → AffIm=1.
If the relationship is purely role-prescribed or transactional with no warmth → AffIm=0.
